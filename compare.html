Comparison guide

A lightweight alternative for the CSV-to-segment slice.

Large agency systems, CRMs, and BI stacks are good at broad workflows. LiteInsurance is smaller on purpose: it helps an ops team turn exported policyholder CSVs into LTV, risk tiers, segments, and campaign math without adding a vendor database.

Which is right for you?

Pick LiteInsurance when

You have policyholder exports, need quick segmentation and cross-sell math, want a portable self-hosted tool, and do not need full policy servicing.

single-file / SQLite / no SaaS database

Pick a bigger stack when

You need producer workflows, policy servicing, carrier integrations, permission models across departments, multi-system reporting, or enterprise support.

CRM / AMS / BI / warehouse
CMP / 03

What you give up

No carrier integrations

LiteInsurance works from CSVs. It does not sync directly with carrier or AMS APIs.

No enterprise workflow suite

There are no queues, approvals, assignments, or field-agent mobile workflows.

No warehouse-scale analytics

SQLite is practical and portable, but it is not a replacement for a governed data warehouse.
